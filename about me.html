Based in Calabar, NG Title: Independent Technologist · Systems Architect

I'm an independent technologist and systems architect based in Nigeria. I design, build, and explore ideas at the intersection of artificial intelligence, cognition, and human potential.

I spend most of my time building — through long periods of focused research, experimentation, and refinement.

How I think.

I'm an engineer by discipline, but a researcher by temperament.

Programming is the medium I use to explore deeper questions: how intelligence works, how systems emerge, and how we can build technology that is not only powerful but understandable.

I have always been drawn to first principles — taking complicated things apart until I understand the foundation, then rebuilding them from the ground up.

My path has been unconventional. I did not follow a traditional academic route into computer science. Instead, I built my education through curiosity, independent study, experimentation, and years of self-directed practical work.

The goal has never been simply to write software.

It has been to understand.

How I build.

Most technology today is optimized for speed, scale, and convenience.

I am interested in something deeper: systems that can be understood, trusted, and easily improved.

With AI my approach is to build from foundations — designing architectures where reasoning, memory, and behavior are deliberate rather than accidental.

The question I keep returning to is:

How do we move from systems that imitate intelligence to systems that genuinely embody it?

That question guides much of my work.

Things
I've been building.

Over the last several years, I have been developing a collection of systems exploring different aspects of intelligence and computation.

AETHER explores fundamentally different architectures for reasoning and machine intelligence.
PAL explores persistent digital identity and human-computer relationships.
Rhodon explores the scientific and analytical applications of my AETHER architecture.
Rubicon-1 represents a broader attempt to push toward a more capable form of human level machine cognition.

These projects are not isolated products. They are experiments in understanding what the next generation of technology will become.

Independent work and why it matters.

Many of history's most significant advances were made possible because someone gave a creator the freedom to pursue difficult questions.

The Renaissance produced some of its greatest works not only because talent existed, but because certain individuals chose to give talent room to develop.

The same model still has relevance today.

Some ideas require time before they become companies, products, or movements. They require the freedom to explore without immediately optimizing for markets.

That is the kind of environment I am trying to create around my work.
