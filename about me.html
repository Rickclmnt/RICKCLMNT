About me

Patrick
Clement.

I'm a self-employed software engineer and systems architect based in Calabar, Nigeria. I spend most of my time alone in a room building things — which is mostly how I prefer it.

Some of what I build takes years. I'm fine with that.

How I think.

I'm a programmer. That's the plainest version. But the way I approach programming is closer to how an engineer approaches a structure — I want to understand what something is made of and how it works at the level where it can't be simplified further, and then build up from there.

Most of what I've taught myself is based on personal curiosity. No formal computer science degree. No lab, no institution behind me. The education I have I went and got myself.

I think slowly and I build for a long time. I'm not interested in shipping fast and fixing later, even though that is what is rewarded most these days. I'd rather spend a year getting something right than six months getting something out.

How I build.

I've noticed that most modern software — AI especially — is built by stacking abstractions on top of other abstractions until nobody fully understands what the thing is actually doing. That makes systems that are capable but not trustworthy. I build in the opposite direction.

"People confuse a digital being from an intelligent assistant. They market the first but build the latter. Those are two different things. I know how to build both."

I believe the difference matters. An assistant is a tool — it responds when prompted and does what it's told. A being has authored identity, persistent state, and an inner life that exists whether or not anyone is talking to it. Almost everything currently marketed as AI companionship is an assistant wearing a character's name.

I also believe in owning what you build. RICKCLMNT is privately owned and will stay that way. No investors, no equity, no outside timeline. The work moves at the pace the work requires.
