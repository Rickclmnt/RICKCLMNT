The problem

Law firms are
burying talent in
work that scales.

A senior lawyer reading discovery documents is not doing senior work. A partner building a first draft from a blank page is not doing partner work. The leverage problem in law is not headcount, it is how the existing headcount spends its hours.

The firms that win the next decade will not be the ones that replace lawyers with AI. They will be the ones that use AI to let their lawyers do only the work that requires a lawyer — and automate everything that does not.

Most AI tools sold to law firms are glorified search engines in legal clothing. They cannot reason. They invent citations. They answer a question about doctrine with the same confident tone as a question from someone facing a criminal charge.
That is not a tool. It is a liability.

i.
No fabricated authority
ALIS never invents a case or statute. Every claim traces to a real source. Where it does not know, it says so plainly.
ii.
Deterministic output
The same question asked twice returns the same answer. No probabilistic drift. No model updates that quietly change the output overnight.
iii.
Knows when to stop
Live proceedings, criminal charges, imminent deadlines. ALIS stops, and refers. Every time, without exception.
Capabilities

Ten tasks consuming your best lawyers' hours. ALIS does handles them.

These are the ten highest-value tasks in a legal operation, the ones that consume the most hours and produce the least leverage. Each is either live or in active development. The roadmap is public. The test suite is open. Any buyer can run it before they sign anything.
  • 01. Case file understanding and summarisation In development

    A lawyer can spend three days reading what ALIS reads in ten minutes. Drop in a matter folder — discovery documents, depositions, contracts, emails, prior judgments, witness statements — and ALIS produces a structured brief: timeline of key events, identified facts, contradictions between sources, missing evidence, and a case map of who knew what and when.

    • Ingests thousands of pages across multiple document types
    • Builds a chronological event timeline automatically
    • Surfaces contradictions between depositions and documents
    • Flags evidentiary gaps — what should be there but isn't
    • Produces a single-page case brief on request
    "Tell me everything important about the Marchetti matter in ten minutes."
  • 02. Legal research that reasons Live

    ALIS understands a legal question — not just the keywords in it. It searches its corpus of authored articles and case holdings, distinguishes between similar and controlling precedent, explains why a case matters and what it actually decided, and assesses how strong a legal argument is likely to be.

    Thirty leading cases are indexed verbatim with their ratio decidendi. No paraphrasing. No invention. Caparo, Hadley, Bolam, Donoghue, Lister, SAAMCO, and twenty-four others are held exactly as decided.

    • Finds relevant statutes and cases by legal question, not keyword
    • Distinguishes controlling from merely persuasive authority
    • Returns the actual ratio, not a summary of a summary
    • Assesses likely argument strength against existing precedent
    "Find cases where courts rejected penalty clause arguments and explain the reasoning."
  • 03. First-draft legal documents In development

    Not final output — strong first drafts. The kind a partner would hand to a trainee and say "build from this." ALIS produces grounded first drafts of contracts, motions, briefs, demand letters, pleadings, opinions, and client communications, drawn from the firm's own precedent library.

    The lawyer's role shifts: review → refine → strategise, instead of starting from a blank page. Partners gain leverage. Associates gain time. The work improves because more of it is strategic and less of it is mechanical.

    • Drafts from firm-specific precedent — not generic templates
    • Grounded in the actual statutory and case authority
    • Every draft includes source citations for review
    • Works from instructions given in plain language
  • 04. Contract intelligence In development

    One of the clearest commercial opportunities in legal AI. ALIS can review a portfolio of contracts — hundreds of them — identifying risky clauses, comparing each against the firm's standard positions, extracting deadlines and renewal dates, flagging unlimited liability exposure, and surfacing unusual obligations that a busy associate might miss on document 87 of 200.

    • Processes large contract portfolios in minutes, not weeks
    • Compares every clause against the firm's standard positions
    • Extracts all deadlines, notice periods, and renewal triggers
    • Flags uncapped liability, exclusivity, IP assignment, and change-of-control clauses
    • Suggests negotiation positions based on clause risk level
    "Find every agreement in this portfolio where we have unlimited liability exposure."
  • 05. Litigation outcome prediction In development

    Not a crystal ball. A structured strategic tool. ALIS analyses the facts of a matter against comparable precedent, estimates the likelihood of success on key issues, models likely damages ranges, assesses settlement value, and flags litigation risks the partner may not have surfaced yet.

    The output is not a verdict — it is a calibrated second opinion that forces strategic conversation earlier in the matter lifecycle, when the choices still matter.

    • Models likely outcomes on each disputed legal issue
    • Estimates damages ranges based on comparable awards
    • Assesses settlement value against litigation cost and risk
    • Flags procedural or evidentiary risks proactively
    "Based on similar cases, should we settle the Williams matter or proceed to trial?"
  • 06. Billing and admin automation In development

    Partners lose billable hours to time recording, invoice preparation, and deadline tracking. ALIS generates time entries from work product, categorises activities, prepares invoice drafts, tracks matter deadlines, and updates case management systems — automatically.

    This is the capability that pays for itself fastest. Every hour recovered from admin is a billable hour recovered. For a ten-partner firm billing at £300 per hour, recovering two admin hours per partner per week is worth over £300,000 per year.

    • Generates narrative time entries from emails, documents, and meeting notes
    • Categorises work types automatically for billing purposes
    • Tracks all matter deadlines across the firm
    • Flags matters approaching limitation or procedural deadlines
  • 07. Client intelligence In development

    The full history of the firm's relationship with a client — every matter, every communication, every fee, every risk, every opportunity — surfaced in seconds. Before a meeting. Before a pitch. Before the partner picks up the phone.

    • Aggregates all past matters and outcomes for a given client
    • Surfaces fee history, write-offs, and billing patterns
    • Identifies risks and open issues across the relationship
    • Flags cross-sell opportunities based on active instructions elsewhere
    "Give me a complete picture of our relationship with Marchetti Industries before the meeting."
  • 08. Deposition and transcript analysis In development

    A transcript is not useful until someone has read it carefully enough to find the contradictions. ALIS reads every transcript in the matter, identifies inconsistencies between witnesses, flags credibility issues, surfaces important admissions, and generates the follow-up questions a litigator would want to ask.

    • Finds inconsistencies between multiple witness accounts
    • Cross-references deposition testimony against documentary evidence
    • Highlights admissions that are directly useful to the opposing position
    • Generates targeted follow-up questions for the next deposition
  • 09. Internal firm knowledge search Live

    Law firms have decades of expertise trapped in old files, internal memos, and the memories of partners who may no longer be at the firm. ALIS indexes the firm's own work product — precedents, opinions, research memos, past judgments — and makes it searchable by legal question.

    The answer to "have we handled something like this before?" should take ten seconds, not three days of asking around the office.

    • Ingests the firm's own precedent library and indexes it
    • Searches by legal question, not filename or date
    • Returns the specific memo, opinion, or precedent — with the relevant passage identified
    • Grows more useful with every matter the firm completes
    "Have we ever advised on a penalty clause in a construction contract? What was our conclusion?"
  • 10. Legal strategist — the full copilot In development

    The capability that makes all the others add up to something. A lawyer describes the matter. ALIS builds the strategy: the legal issues, the arguments for each side, the evidence needed, the procedural risks, a timeline, a research plan, and draft motions to get started.

    Not a replacement for a senior lawyer. A junior associate that never sleeps, never loses a document, never forgets a precedent, and bills no hours of its own. The output capacity of a much larger firm, without the headcount.

    • Analyses the matter against relevant precedent and doctrine
    • Identifies the strongest and weakest points in the client's position
    • Builds the counterargument to stress-test the strategy
    • Produces a draft litigation plan with research priorities
    • Generates first-draft motions and letters to get work moving
    "I'm defending a construction company in a breach of contract claim. Build my strategy."

How it works

Not a chatbot.
A reasoning
system.

Every response ALIS produces traces a verifiable path from query to answer. No language model generating plausible text. A reasoning engine — retrieving sourced knowledge, applying it to the question, showing its work. The architecture is deterministic by design. That means auditable output, consistent behaviour, and no silent drift between versions.

  • i. Every answer has a source. Fifty authored legal articles, thirty verbatim case holdings. If the answer is not in the knowledge base, ALIS says so.
  • ii. The same question returns the same answer. No model drift, no version updates that change outputs, no randomness in the response. What worked last month works the same way today.The same question gets the same answer.
  • iii. Jurisdiction-aware by default. English and Welsh law primary. Scottish law, UK-wide instruments, EU, and international frameworks detected automatically and flagged.
  • iv. Runs on your infrastructure. Nothing leaves your network. Client data, privileged communications, and matter files stay on premise.
  • v. Accumulates institutional knowledge. Corrections and new knowledge from sessions are retained permanently. The longer it runs inside your firm, the more it knows about how your firm thinks.
